I work as a postdoctoral researcher at the Astronomical Institute of the Czech Academy of Sciences, in the Relativistic Astrophysics Group. My research interests lie in the field of gravitational waves, mainly in the context of the upcoming LISA mission, namely:

  • LISA data analysis,
  • modeling of EMRIs, dynamical systems,
  • machine learning searches in LIGO data.

I have completed my bachelor's and master's studies at the Faculty of Mathematics and Physics, Charles University in Prague, both theses aimed at potential LISA sources. I have obtained my doctorate from the Friedrich Schiller University in Jena, Germany, where my dissertation focused on applications of machine learning methods to analyze LIGO data.

You can find my publications on INSPIRE-HEP or NASA/ADS.
